About Me
========

As a second-year Ph.D. student in Dr. Bradley Voytek's lab, my research is focused on unraveling the intricate relationship between peripheral signals and cortical dynamics. Specifically, I am keenly interested in investigating the impact of cardiac signals on scalp electroencephalography (EEG). By delving into the nuances of how cardiac activity influences neural processes, I aim to contribute to our understanding of the complex interplay between physiological signals and brain function. This line of inquiry gains additional significance when considered across the lifespan, given the well-documented age-related changes in both the neural and cardiac systems.

Beyond my scientific pursuits, I am an active member of the neuroscientific community, advocating for open science practices and fostering public science literacy. My commitment extends to promoting greater female engagement in STEM fields, aligning with my passion for inclusivity and diversity within scientific disciplines.

By combining my research focus on the intersection of peripheral signals and cortical dynamics with my advocacy for open science and inclusivity, I strive to contribute not only to advancements in our understanding of the brain but also to the broader goals of promoting transparency, accessibility, and diversity in scientific endeavors.
